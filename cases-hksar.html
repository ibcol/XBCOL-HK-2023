Use Cases by HKSAR Industry Verticals

Read me if you are competing in HKBCOL 2023

Support of Government Policies

As the world gradually becomes more digitalised, a transition accelerated by years of pandemic-induced physical separation, trust technologies are taking an increasingly prominent role in our daily lives. In the 2022 Policy Address, the Chief Executive of Hong Kong has highlighted several key industries that are instrumental to the socioeconomic development of Hong Kong by directly increasing or indirectly influencing the quality of life and the growth of the economy. These cases are the focus of the 2023 Hong Kong Blockchain Olympiad.

Fintech Contech Proptech Edtech ArtTech

Fintech

Ripple, a real-time gross settlement system and currency exchange network that uses blockchain technology to provide secure, fast, and low-cost cross-border payments. Ripple uses its own cryptocurrency, XRP, as a bridge currency to facilitate cross-border payments between different currencies. The use of blockchain technology enables Ripple to settle transactions in real-time, providing greater speed and efficiency compared to traditional payment systems. Additionally, Ripple's use of blockchain technology provides a secure and transparent way of tracking payments, reducing the risk of fraud and increasing accountability. Furthermore, Ripple's low transaction fees make it an attractive alternative to traditional payment systems, particularly for cross-border payments where fees can be high. By using blockchain technology, Ripple is creating a more efficient, secure, and accessible Fintech ecosystem that has the potential to transform the way we approach cross-border payments.

Contech

Provenance, a platform that uses blockchain technology to increase transparency and traceability in the construction industry. Provenance enables construction materials to be tracked throughout the entire supply chain, from raw materials to finished products, using a tamper-proof digital ledger. This provides a more efficient and transparent way of managing the construction supply chain, reducing the risk of fraud and ensuring that materials are sourced sustainably and ethically. Additionally, Provenance enables construction companies to verify the quality and safety of materials, reducing the risk of defects or safety hazards in buildings. Furthermore, Provenance uses blockchain technology to create a secure and transparent platform for managing construction contracts, ensuring that all parties are held accountable for their actions and that contracts are executed efficiently and transparently. By using blockchain technology, Provenance is creating a more sustainable, efficient, and trustworthy Contech ecosystem.

Proptech

Propy, a real estate transaction platform that uses blockchain technology to provide a secure and transparent way of buying and selling properties globally. Propy uses smart contracts to automate the buying and selling process, enabling parties to complete transactions in a matter of days, rather than weeks or months. Smart contracts also provide a secure and transparent way of managing escrow, allowing parties to track the status of funds and ensuring that funds are only released when certain conditions are met. Additionally, Propy uses blockchain technology to create a tamper-proof record of all property transactions, enabling parties to verify the ownership and history of a property. This reduces the risk of fraud and provides greater transparency in the property market. Furthermore, Propy uses blockchain technology to enable cross-border property transactions, which can be particularly challenging due to varying regulations and legal systems. By using blockchain technology, Propy is creating a more efficient, transparent, and trustworthy Proptech ecosystem.

Edtech

Learning Machine, a blockchain-based digital credentialing platform that enables institutions to issue and verify academic records in a secure and tamper-proof manner. Learning Machine uses the Blockcerts open standard, which allows for the creation and verification of secure, decentralized digital credentials on the blockchain. With Learning Machine, educational institutions can issue digital certificates that can be verified by employers, other educational institutions, or any other interested parties in real-time, without the need for intermediaries. This provides a more efficient, transparent, and secure way of verifying academic credentials, reducing the risk of fraud and making it easier for graduates to enter the job market. Additionally, Learning Machine enables students to have greater control over their academic records and to share them in a more secure and efficient manner, while also maintaining their privacy. By using blockchain technology, Learning Machine is creating a more transparent, efficient, and trustworthy Edtech ecosystem.

ArtTech

Maecenas, a decentralized art gallery that enables art collectors to buy and sell shares in famous artworks. Maecenas uses blockchain technology to create tamper-proof certificates of ownership, called "smart contracts", which are stored on the Ethereum blockchain. These smart contracts provide a secure and transparent way for investors to buy and sell shares in artworks, with the ownership of the artwork being tracked and verified through the blockchain. Maecenas also uses blockchain technology to create a transparent and auditable record of all art transactions, providing a high level of accountability and reducing the risk of fraud in the art market. Through the use of blockchain technology, Maecenas is creating a more democratic and accessible art market, allowing more people to invest in and enjoy high-value artwork.
